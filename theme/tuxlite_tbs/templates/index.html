About

Scientific Software Days is an ongoing meeting of users and producers of scientific software, with presentations by scientific software tool makers and the users of their tools. The intent is to develop skills and networks among scientific software developers and practitioners. Presentations are split between corss-cutting software tools and stories of of successful software deployment in various disciplines. Commonly, less than optimal paths are taken in scientific software development, largely because of resources and skills stretched between the relevant science specialties and professional programming. Available tools and methos\ds are not always known or available to the people who need them. We seek to build a community to address these needs.

Keynote

The Seventh Annual Software Days keynote address will be given by Neil Chue Hong, director of the Software Sustainability Institute of the University of Edinburgh.

Sponsors

Scientific Software Days is sponsored by the Jackson School of Geosciences, Texas Advanced Computing Center, and the Insitute for Computational Engineering and Sciences, with funding from XSEDE, the Extreme Science and Engineering Discovery Environment program.
